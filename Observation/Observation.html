THIEN VAN OBSERVATION

An observatory or observatory, astronomical observatory, astronomical observatory is a work equipped with telescopes and other necessary equipment to observe and track celestial objects in the sky. heaven, or natural phenomena on Earth such as meteorology.

Observatory observatories are key structures in astronomical research, providing collaborative work between astronomers, giving them access to state-of-the-art equipment and telescopes to meet their needs. The demand for observation is increasingly precise and urgent.

OBSERVATORY

Gemini Observatory:This observatory has two optical and infrared telescopes installed at two distant locations. The Gemini South telescope is installed at an altitude of 9,000 feet (2,700 meters) in the Chilean Andes, while the North telescope, mounted at the summit of Maura Kea volcano, is capable of seeing the sky at night.

The European Southern Observatory (ESO): ESO consists of a high-tech telescope and is also the world's first telescope with a computer-controlled mirror. Very modern, working in collaboration with other observatories in North America, East Asia and Chile, it is considered the most modern radio astronomy observatory in the world today.

National Radio Astronomy Observatory (NRAD): This is a cluster of American observatories installed in many places, including the Green Bank Telescope, Very Large Array, Very Large Baseline Array and a part of the ALMA telescope that is about to be put into use. The NRAD station is responsible for the continuous observation of meteorites passing through space and interstellar space.

Mount Wilson Observatory: The birth of the Mount Wilson observatory is considered an evolutionary process of generations of telescopes, especially the birth of a new modern instrument such as a 100-inch wide glass instead of the 60-inch Hale Telescope. nearly 100 years ago and thanks to being equipped with many modern equipment..
