What is Planet Moon?

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest natural satellite in the Solar System. So far, this is also the only celestial body other than Earth that humans have set foot on.

Most astronomers agree that the Moon was formed 4.527 ± 0.01 billion years ago, that is, about 30 - 50 million years after the Solar System was formed. By far the most dominant theory of the formation of the Moon is the big impact hypothesis. Accordingly, a Mars-sized object is thought to have crashed into the proto-Earth, ejecting a large enough amount of matter into Earth's orbit and forming the Moon through accretion..
The Moon has a very thin, barely noticeable atmosphere. The total mass of its atmosphere is less than 10^4 kg. With such a thin atmosphere, the surface of the Moon cannot keep the temperature, so the average temperature here is 107 degrees Celsius during the day and -153 degrees Celsius at night.

  • 2018 - 2021: Each of the eclipse cycles repeats every 177 days and 4 hours (one cycle) at alternating intersections of the Moon's orbit.
  • Day 3 and 4.12: According to timeanddate.com, when a full moon or a new moon occurs at the time when the Moon is closest to Earth, called a perihelion, it is a supermoon.
  • Super new moon on December 4, 2021 occurs when the Moon and Earth are at a distance of 356,804 km and is the second super new moon of the year, after the super new moon on November 4 (when the Sun and Moon are 359,856 km apart).
  • For astronomy lovers who want to really see the giant Moon, simply observe the Moon as it rises or sets. Because the Moon near the horizon will always be very large thanks to the famous phenomenon known as the Moon illusion.

ON THE MOON

The moon's surface is covered with dead volcanoes, impact craters, and lava flows, some visible to the unaided stargazer. Early scientists thought the dark stretches of the moon might be oceans, and so named such features mare, which is Latin for "seas" (maria when there are more than one).

THE MOON ROUND

The Moon takes about one month to orbit Earth (27.3 days to complete a revolution, but 29.5 days to change from New Moon to New Moon). As the Moon completes each 27.3-day orbit around Earth, both Earth and the Moon are moving around the Sun.
